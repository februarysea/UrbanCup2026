# Topic

## Working Title

Rainstorm-Induced Travel Behavior Shifts in New York City: A Multi-Agent Simulation Using Bike, Taxi, and Subway Mobility Data

## City Problem

Rainstorms disrupt short-distance urban travel. Bike trips become less attractive or unsafe, while subway and taxi systems may absorb substitution demand unevenly across space. The resulting mobility loss is not only an efficiency issue; it can expose inequalities between zones with strong multimodal access and zones with weak alternatives.

## Research Question

How do New York City travelers change mode choices before, during, and after rainstorms, and which policy interventions reduce trip cancellation, delay, and unequal access to alternatives?

## Modeling Position

Real mobility data is used to estimate observed storm responses. AgentSociety2 is used to represent heterogeneous traveler decision mechanisms and test counterfactual policies that cannot be observed directly from historical data.
