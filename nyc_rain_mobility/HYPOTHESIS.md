# Hypotheses

H1. Rainstorm hours significantly reduce Citi Bike trips, with a larger reduction among casual or leisure-like users than commuter-like users.

H2. Suppressed bike demand is only partially substituted by taxi and subway trips; a nontrivial share is delayed or cancelled.

H3. Subway-accessible zones are more likely to shift from bike to subway during rainstorms.

H4. Zones with stronger taxi supply are more likely to shift from bike to taxi during rainstorms.

H5. Low-alternative-access zones experience higher cancellation and delay rates, revealing spatial inequality in transport resilience.

H6. Early weather warnings, transit guidance, and targeted taxi supply support reduce travel disruption through different mechanisms and create different system-pressure tradeoffs.
